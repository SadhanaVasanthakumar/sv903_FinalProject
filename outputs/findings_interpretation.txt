FINDING 1 -- AI Agency Framing (PSI)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Over 20 months (Aug 2024 - Mar 2026), the Power Shift Index
decreased at -10.604 points/month (R-squared = 0.278,
p = 0.0170).
A negative slope indicates that AI-agent framing declined relative
to human-agent framing over the observation window. This likely
reflects growing coverage of human responses to AI -- regulation,
policy debates, research -- outpacing pure AI-as-actor coverage.

Why this matters for AI discourse:
Media framing shapes how readers attribute responsibility. Sentences
like 'AI made a decision' implicitly absolve the humans who built and
deployed the system. Sentences like 'engineers configured the system to
decide' restore agency to humans. Tracking this ratio over time reveals
structural shifts in how the public discusses accountability for AI.

FINDING 2 -- Anxiety in AI Coverage
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Mean AAI across the corpus is 0.037 on a [0,1] scale.
Safety & Risk headlines have AAI 0.093 vs Companies &
Products at 0.018 (Cohen's d = +0.749, 
p = 0.00e+00). The effect size is medium.

Why this matters:
Anxiety is concentrated in the Safety & Risk topic-tier as expected,
but it is not absent from product coverage. Even routine company news
retains some anxiety vocabulary, suggesting the broader news ecosystem
treats AI as inherently risk-adjacent.

nuanced middle-ground framing.

FINDING 5 -- The Invisible Human (refined)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
92.4% of all 179K AI headlines contain ZERO vocabulary
from any human-experience domain (grief, spirituality, love,
bodily experience, dignity, community, childhood).

Even conditioning on headlines that mention humans (workers,
researchers, users, etc.), only 6.8% engage human-experience
domains. The absence is not just topical -- it is structural.

Why this matters:
AI is increasingly described in news as if its human consequences
are technical or economic, not experiential. The dimensions of human
life that ethics and philosophy treat as central -- grief, meaning,
dignity, love -- are absent even when humans are explicitly
the subject of AI coverage.

FINDING 6 -- Predictive Modeling (with honesty notes)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Ridge regression on AAI achieves R-squared = 0.067 after
removing circular features (bias dimensions sharing vocabulary
with AAI components). Removing those features reduced R-squared
from 0.220 to 0.067, indicating the full-feature
score was inflated by vocabulary overlap rather than predictive signal.

Decision tree classification of dominant_bias achieves
