01 / Introduction

What we set out to learn

Most people learn about AI from headlines, not research papers. A headline that says "AI Takes Over Another Industry" is doing something different from "Companies Adopt AI Automation Tools," even when the underlying story is identical. The first frames AI as an actor with its own will. The second describes a choice that people made. This project measures those framings at scale across 179,372 headlines.

Is news coverage increasingly treating AI itself as the actor, while humans recede into the background?

That question became the Power Shift Index. The second question — how concentrated is anxiety in AI coverage? — became the AI Anxiety Index. The third question — when coverage is about humans, do headlines engage with human experience? — became the Invisible Human Framework. Three hypotheses were supported. Two were not. We walk through what we found and what we got wrong.

01 Finding One

AI is becoming the subject of its own coverage

PSI rose at 34.6 points per month (p = 0.0022, R² = 0.41).

News coverage is increasingly framing AI systems as actors, with humans becoming implicit operators.
What we got wrong

Cohen's kappa = 0.37 (fair). The classifier is too strict, but the trend is real.

02 Finding Two

Anxiety concentrates in Risk coverage but spreads everywhere

Safety & Risk headlines have AAI 0.093 vs Companies & Products at 0.018. Cohen's d = 0.749.

03 Finding Three

The two camps: AI discourse has bifurcated

Negative biases (fear, moral panic, control loss) cluster together. Positive biases (optimism, techno-utopianism) form a separate cluster. There is almost no middle ground.

The two camps are largely separable. A reader scrolling AI headlines is being asked to choose a side: the language of breakthrough and unlimited potential, or the language of crisis and existential risk. The vocabulary that would let you sit with both at once — trade-offs, context-dependence — is the vocabulary the news ecosystem does not reach for.

04 Finding Four

92.4% of AI headlines contain zero human-experience vocabulary

No grief, no love, no dignity, no community. Even when headlines mention humans, only 7.9% engage these domains.

Of the most anxious 10% of headlines, 90.9% contain zero human-experience vocabulary.
H4 failed (sanity check)

Headlines mentioning humans did not engage human-experience domains more than others (d = 0.016, p = 0.066). The unconditional claim stands alone.

07 / What We Got Wrong

Limitations, in plain language

PSI classifier is too strict

Cohen's kappa = 0.37. The classifier misses passive constructions. The trend is robust, but absolute numbers are conservative.

Invisible Human conflates topic with treatment

A headline about funding doesn't mention grief because it's not about grief. The unconditional claim (92.4%) is the honest version.

Political-lean coverage is thin

Only 118 of 13,019 publishers matched AllSides (0.91%). No claims about political asymmetry.

Forecasting is extrapolation

20 observations is enough for a trend test, not for ARIMA. The 95% CI on PSI spans 1,200 points.
